Claude Money Prompts

Claude can fix your whole money situation — this weekend.

10 copy-paste prompts that turn Claude into your free financial planner — from mapping new income to killing debt to automating your savings. Fill in the blanks and every answer is about YOU, not generic advice.

How to use: ① Open claude.ai (free). ② Copy a prompt. ③ Replace the <yellow bits> with your own numbers. ④ Paste & send. The more honest your details, the sharper the plan.
The 10 prompts

1 Income Stream Mapper

Realistic ways to earn more, matched to your background

You are a financial strategist. Based on my background, map realistic ways I could earn additional income. My situation: - Current job / skills: <e.g. marketing coordinator, strong at writing & design> - Hours I can spare per week: <e.g. 8> - Starting capital available: <e.g. $500> - Goal: <e.g. an extra $1,000/month within 6 months> Give me 7 realistic income streams ranked by startup difficulty (1-10), time to first dollar, income ceiling, and fit with my skills. For each, list the exact first 3 steps to start this week. Then flag the 2 best fits for me and why.

2 Debt Destruction Engine

The fastest, cheapest route to zero debt

Act as a debt-payoff strategist. Here are my debts: <list each — name, balance, interest rate, minimum payment. e.g. Credit card, $4,200, 22%, $120> Extra amount I can put toward debt each month: <e.g. $300> Compare the avalanche (highest interest first) vs snowball (smallest balance first) methods for MY debts. Show total interest paid, months to debt-free, and a month-by-month payoff order for each. Tell me which saves the most money, which keeps me most motivated, and the exact payment to send each account every month.

3 Salary Negotiation Script

A word-for-word script to get a raise

You are a compensation negotiation coach. Help me get a raise. - Role & industry: <e.g. software engineer, fintech> - Location: <e.g. Austin, TX> - Years of experience: <e.g. 3> - Current salary: <e.g. $95,000> - Recent wins: <list 2-3 achievements> Give me: (1) a realistic target range for my role and market, (2) a word-for-word script to open the conversation with my manager, (3) confident responses to the 3 most likely pushbacks ("no budget", "not the right time", "let's revisit later"), and (4) my walk-away options. Confident, not arrogant.

4 Wealth Automation System

Auto-route every paycheck so you save without thinking

Act as a personal-finance systems designer. Build me an automated money system so I save and invest on autopilot. - Monthly take-home pay: <e.g. $4,500> - Fixed expenses: <e.g. $2,600> - Current savings / investments: <e.g. $3,000> - Goals: <e.g. 6-month emergency fund, then invest long-term> Design a "paycheck routing" system: exactly what % or $ auto-transfers to each bucket (bills, emergency fund, investing, guilt-free spending) the day I'm paid. Give me the account structure, the automation rules, and the order to fund each bucket — simple enough to set up in one afternoon.

5 Budget & Expense Tracker

A real budget + where your money is leaking

You are a budgeting coach. Build me a simple, realistic budget and find where my money leaks. - Take-home income (monthly): <e.g. $4,500> - Rent / mortgage: <e.g. $1,400> - Other recurring bills: <utilities, phone, insurance, etc.> - Rough spend on food / transport / fun: <estimate each> Build a monthly budget using the 50/30/20 framework adapted to MY numbers, as a clear table. Flag the 3 categories where I'm likely overspending vs typical benchmarks, estimate how much I could realistically free up, and give me one simple rule to fix each.

6 Subscription Assassin

Find and kill the recurring charges you forgot about

Act as a subscription auditor. Here are my recurring charges (from my card / bank statement): <paste the recurring lines, or list each subscription + monthly cost> Sort them into KEEP (clear value), CUT NOW (unused / forgotten / duplicate), and DOWNGRADE (cheaper tier or annual billing saves money). Total up how much I'd save per month and per year if I act. Then write a 2-line cancellation checklist for the top 3 to cut.

7 Emergency Fund Blueprint

How much, where to keep it, how fast to build it

You are a financial planner. Build my emergency fund plan. - Essential monthly expenses (rent, food, bills, minimums): <e.g. $3,000> - Job stability: <stable salaried / freelance / commission> - Current emergency savings: <e.g. $800> - Amount I can save per month: <e.g. $400> Tell me: (1) how many months of expenses I should hold and the exact dollar target for MY situation, (2) how long to get there at my savings rate, (3) what type of account to keep it in so it's safe but earning something, and (4) 3 ways to hit the target faster. End with a month-by-month savings schedule.

8 Side-Hustle Matcher

Your skills → realistic side incomes, ranked

Act as a side-income advisor. Match my specific skills and constraints to realistic side hustles. - Skills / things I'm good at: <list> - Time available per week: <e.g. 6 hours> - Online-only or local/in-person: <choose> - Income goal: <e.g. $500-800/month> Give me 6 side hustles that fit ME specifically, ranked by realistic $/hour, speed to first dollar, and startup cost. For my top 2, write the exact first-week action plan and where to find my first customer. Be realistic — no get-rich-quick.

9 Big-Purchase Reality Check

Can you actually afford it? Green / yellow / red

You are a financial decision analyst. Help me decide if I can truly afford a big purchase. - The purchase: <e.g. a $28,000 car / an apartment at $1,900/mo / a $3,000 trip> - Monthly take-home: <e.g. $4,500> - Current monthly expenses: <e.g. $3,200> - Savings: <e.g. $9,000> - Existing debt payments: <e.g. $250> Show the true all-in cost (including hidden / ongoing costs), the hit to my monthly cash flow and savings rate, and a clear GREEN / YELLOW / RED verdict for my situation. If it's yellow or red, give me 3 smarter alternatives and the income or savings level where it becomes a green.

10 5-Year Money Roadmap

Goals → sequenced milestones → what to do this week

Act as a financial planner and build my 5-year money roadmap. - Age & situation: <e.g. 29, single, renting> - Income: <e.g. $60,000> - Savings / investments: <e.g. $5,000> - Debt: <e.g. $12,000 student loans> - Big goals: <e.g. emergency fund, pay off loans, buy a home, start investing> Turn my goals into a sequenced 5-year plan — what to tackle first, second, third and WHY (order matters). For each year give me specific dollar milestones, the one monthly habit that gets me there, and one thing to avoid. End with a "next 7 days" checklist so I start today.

That's your weekend sorted.
