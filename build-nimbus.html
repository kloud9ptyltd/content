Cloud9 × Nimbus

Build your own JARVIS for the markets.

Nimbus is a voice AI market terminal. You talk to it and it answers out loud — in a calm British voice — with live market data, on a cinematic Iron-Man-style HUD: a reactive glowing orb, live panels, a market galaxy you can fly through. It runs on a small home server or a cheap VPS. Here's the exact stack.

🎙️ Talk to it🧠 Claude brain🔊 ElevenLabs voice 📈 Live market data🪐 Cinematic HUD
The stack — five pieces

1 The backend

A tiny FastAPI (Python) app. It serves the HUD as a single web page and exposes a few endpoints: one to ask a question, one for text-to-speech, and a couple for the live market feed. That's the whole spine — a few hundred lines.

2 The brain — Claude

Every question goes to Claude (via the Anthropic API). The trick isn't the model — it's the context: you fetch the live numbers first, hand them to Claude, and it narrates a tight, witty spoken answer. Give it a persona (a British butler who runs a hedge fund) and it delivers a proper morning brief on command.

Speaking out loud means no markdown, no bullet points — short, punchy sentences that sound good read aloud.

3 The voice — ElevenLabs

The magic is the delivery. ElevenLabs' API turns Claude's text into a warm, expressive British voice in about a second. Pick a voice you like, send the text, stream back the audio, and play it in the browser. Their low-latency model keeps the back-and-forth feeling like a real conversation.

You'll need an ElevenLabs API key with a voice-generation plan that fits your usage.

4 The market data

Real numbers, from free-ish market sources: quotes and company news, plus indices, crypto, gold and the VIX. This is what Claude narrates — indices, today's biggest movers, your portfolio, the headlines that matter.

5 The HUD

One self-contained HTML page with an HTML canvas: a reactive particle orb that pulses to the voice, live market panels, a news crawl, and a full-screen 3D market galaxy you can orbit and fly through. Pure canvas + a little WebGL — no heavy framework.

How it fits together
  1. You tap the orb and speak → the browser does speech-to-text.
  2. The backend fetches the live market data for your question.
  3. Claude turns the data + your question into a spoken answer.
  4. ElevenLabs voices it; the browser plays it and the orb reacts.

⚠️ The one rule that matters: the AI must never invent a number. A market brief with a wrong price is worse than none. The script fetches the figures; the model only writes the words around them. Grounded, always.

One gotcha: browser microphones need a secure context, so serve the HUD over HTTPS (a self-signed cert on your LAN is fine).
