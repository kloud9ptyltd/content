The call

Investment thesis

Long MU — the AI memory super-cycle turned DRAM from a boom-bust commodity into a supply-constrained, pricing-power business, and Micron is 1 of only 3 players who can supply HBM. The market is paying just ~8× forward earnings for triple-digit EPS growth.
Why it works

Key pillars

✓
HBM demand is structural. Every AI accelerator needs stacks of high-bandwidth memory — supply is sold out ahead.
✓
DRAM oligopoly. Micron / Samsung / SK Hynix only — disciplined capacity = durable pricing.
✓
Earnings inflection. EPS $44 → ~$141 forward; fat margins (gross 58%, operating 48%, net 41%).
✓
Valuation gap. ~8× forward P/E for a business growing revenue triple digits off the trough.
✓
Policy tailwind. US fab incentives + tariffs on Asian rivals.
The falsifiers

Key risks

!
It's still a cycle. Memory always over-builds; today's −6.7% is the reminder.
!
Forward-loaded valuation. ~8× only works if forward EPS scales toward ~$141 — if the cycle rolls over, it re-rates hard.
!
HBM competition. Samsung / SK Hynix yield catch-up compresses share & margins.
!
AI capex digestion. If hyperscaler spend pauses, memory demand pauses with it.
